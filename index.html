Frequently Asked Questions

General & Functional

TAMS CMS is the Commercial Management System module of the Total Airport Management Suite. It delivers a centralized airport billing platform for both aeronautical and non‑aeronautical revenues, combining contract & tariff management with integrations to operational systems and ERP to produce accurate, auditable invoices.

Yes. TAMS CMS supports end‑to‑end billing for aeronautical services (e.g., landing, parking, PBB/400Hz/PCA/PWC usage) and non‑aeronautical streams (e.g., rent, utilities, revenue share from retail/POS).

The CMS tariff engine models complex rules—minimum guarantees, percentage‑of‑turnover, discounts/surcharges, effective periods, and prioritization—so that concession agreements and airline contracts are executed automatically and consistently. Real examples include formulas that combine POS turnover with minimum guarantees or parent usages.

Absolutely. CMS can run for a single regional airport or as a multi‑airport, multi‑language deployment operated centrally, aligning with TAMS' modular, enterprise architecture.

By correlating actual operational usage (from FMS/RMS and service logs) with the contractual rules and pushing the results into ERP for timely invoicing, CMS minimizes human error, improves data consistency across systems, and reduces dispute rates.

Technical & Integration

CMS receives flight and resource usage from TAMS FMS/AODB and RMS (e.g., stand/gate/counter assignments and service usages) so billing is based on what actually happened, not on estimates.

CMS integrates with well‑known ERPs such as SAP and Oracle. It transfers invoice records and can also receive master data (organizations, customers, terms, currencies) via standard XML/JSON interfaces.

Yes. For aeronautical events, CMS processing leverages IATA Type‑B messages; broader TAMS integrations use AIDX‑style exchanges through the TAMS integration layer.

POS transactions (amount, timestamp, location, etc.) flow one‑way from POS to CMS. Tariff rules then compute concession fees, including minimum annual guarantees or tiered percentages, ensuring transparent revenue‑share calculations.

TAMS is cloud‑ready and also supports on‑premises deployments—giving operators flexibility to meet their infrastructure and data‑sovereignty requirements.

CMS and the wider TAV Technologies portfolio operate under recognized standards (e.g., ISO 27001 for information security). CMS provides detailed audit trails for contract, tariff, and billing actions to support compliance and finance controls.

Financial & Revenue Management

Accuracy comes from automated data gathering (flight messages, resource allocations, service usages, passenger loads), rule‑driven tariffs, and data consistency checks before posting to ERP—closing the gap between operations and finance.

The tariff engine supports unlimited, configurable rules across currencies, periods, thresholds, and metrics (e.g., MTOW, aircraft group, time bands, POS turnover). Administrators can define formulas and priorities per service type.

CMS includes invoicing and revenue reports out‑of‑the‑box and plugs into TAMS dashboards for operational‑financial KPIs—so commercial performance can be tracked alongside operations.

Yes. ERP‑aligned reference structures for currency, conversion, organizations, revenue codes, and customer transaction types ensure that CMS calculations and invoices comply with local finance and tax regulations.

Common Challenges & How CMS Helps

CMS acts as the commercial hub in the TAMS architecture—consolidating operational usage (FMS/RMS), applying business rules, and synchronizing with ERP—so all stakeholders work from a single, consistent source of truth.

By combining POS feeds, rental/utility usages, and contract rules (e.g., turnover‑based fees with minimum guarantees), CMS provides transparent revenue‑share billing and insights that highlight underperformance or upside opportunities.

The UI is browser‑based, supports multi‑language, UTC/local time switching, configurable grids, and approval workflows—reducing training effort and improving adoption across departments and locations.
